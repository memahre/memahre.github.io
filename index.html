Case Study: Communication & Health Behavior (Survey Evaluation)

Problem

Health behaviors are shaped not only by individual psychology, but also by how information is communicated. This project evaluated how communication and behavioral theory–informed messaging related to changes in health attitudes/behaviors.

Data

Pre- and post-test survey data from approximately 500 students.

Method

Study design and variable construction; quantitative analysis of behavioral and communication measures; interpretation using behavioral change theories.

Outcome

Produced a research-driven evaluation of patterns in survey responses and connected results back to behavioral theory. (Public artifacts forthcoming.)
